Case Study: Fraudulent Online Payments

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a rapidly evolving type of cybercrime, where an attacker trick users into making payments or revealing highly sensitive financial information. Most commonly involved in this type of fraud are phishing links, fake customer service calls, and QR code scams.

The way this fraud works is that a victim receives a call or message from someone claiming to represent a bank, payment application, or service. The attacker creates a certain level of urgency by indicating that the victim's account will be frozen or that he/she will be issued a refund. The victim is instructed by the attacker to click a link that is actually a malware-infested site, give up an OTP (one-time password), and/or scan a QR code. In many situations, the victim believes they are using a QR code to receive funds, however, scanning the QR code actually allows the payment to be completed. By providing the necessary information or taking the requested actions, the victim immediately transfers their money to the fraudster's account.

Typically, those targeted by this crime have little or no knowledge of payment security when it comes to digital payments. Generally, this group includes students, older generations, and first-time UPI users. Consequences often include loss of money; stress from loss of money, and loss of confidence in digital payments.

To prevent such fraud, users must never share OTPs, avoid clicking on unknown links, and verify the authenticity of calls and messages. Awareness and caution are key to staying safe in digital transactions.
